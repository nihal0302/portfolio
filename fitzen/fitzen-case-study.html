PROBLEM

Working professionals fail to keep themselves healthy and fit.

After working for about 4 years in corporate industry, I noticed one trend that a lot of people start developing health related issues and some negligence towards it.

An expert’s knowledge & inputs are more likely to help you in achieving your fitness goals.

PROJECT GOALS

I am creating an app to help people hire a certified and reliable fitness trainer.

Through the app users can hire a trainer for personal training, group training or online training sessions at their home or at the gym so that users who have a very tight schedule or users who have low budget can also hire a trainer and achieve their fitness goals.

USER RESEARCH

I interviewed 4 people who have either a 9-5 job or they are into a business to earn their living.

The intention of the interviews was to understand how they balance their health along with work. Below are the few sample questions which I asked them during the interview.

Sample Questions

(i) What is your definition of a healthy/fit person? Do you consider yourself fit?
(ii) How often do you workout? Do you go to the gym or you prefer home workouts? And why?
(iii) Do you workout alone or with some friends?
(iv) How about if someone else is also responsible for your fitness?
(v) What factors would you consider before hiring a fitness trainer?
(vi) What is refraining you from getting a fitness trainer?

Insights from the interviews

(i) 3 out of 4 users do not work out on a regular basis and says that they do not have time to workout.
(ii) 2 out of 4 users have gym membership. But due to lack of motivation and time they are not regular to the gym.
(iii) 2 out of 4 have small gym and a swimming pool in their society.
(iv) Fear of being judged by others in the gym for the way they look or for doing something incorrectly.
(v) Having a partner or an accountable person matters a lot when it comes to being regular in workouts.
(vi) Trainers should be motivating, experienced and should understand their client’s fitness goals. They should have happy and satisfied clients from the past.
(vii) Fitness trainers are expensive and users don't know if they can trust the trainers for their fitness goals..

COMPETITIVE AUDIT

1. Fitnesstrainer (Direct Competitor)

GOOD

(i) Provides both 1-on-1 training in person and online training.
(ii) Certified trainers with reviews from past clients is available on the website.
(iii) Training location and travel radius is available for each trainer.
(iv) Website is simple and easy to navigate

BAD

(i) No dedicated app. Only available on mobile and desktop website.
(ii) No real time slots availability of trainers. Only general available slots are mentioned.
(iii) No information for Group training sessions
(iv) No options to filter out trainers on the basis of their speciality.

2. Superprof (Indirect Competitor)

GOOD

(i) Provides both 1-on-1 training in person and online training.
(ii) Special tags given to good trainers/teachers.
(iii) No information for Group training sessions.
(iv) Option to search for sports specific trainers/teachers.

BAD

(i) No dedicated app. Only available on mobile and desktop website.
(ii) Not sure if trainers/teachers certifications are authentic or not.
(iii) No information on how much the trainer is willing to travel
(iv) No information on general or real time slot availabilty.

3. Cult Fit (Indirect Competitor)

GOOD

(i) Dedicated app, mobile website and desktop website available.
(ii) One stop solution for multiple minor health related issues.
(iii) Live online training sessions
(iv) Visually appealing website and app.

BAD

(i) No 1-on-1 training available. Only at-center group sessions available.
(ii) No information on size of at-center group sessions.
(iii) No information on trainers certifications or past clients and their reviews.
(iv) At times a user might feel that, too much is going-on on their screen and can feel overwhelmed.

LEARNINGS

The first case study I've ever done has come to a successful conclusion after 10 weeks of hard work.Throughout the design process, I appreciated how there were different challenges and how each step helped me learn something new.

Nevertheless, in the end one always feels there are a few things that could be improved.

Below is a list of those few things that I would definitely improve in my future work .


Do not decide the target users on the basis of your assumptions but conduct user survey and similar activities on the large number of audience to find out your targeted user group.
Discuss whatever decisions that you have taken with someone else before implementing it in your designs. That means get feedback! As much as possible.
Proper documentation in every step of design process. So at the time of building case study you don’t miss anything.
Set time limit for each tasks and try to plan 3-4 days of your work ahead of time.
